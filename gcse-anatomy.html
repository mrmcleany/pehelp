Functions of the Skeleton

The skeleton has five main functions:

  • Support – gives the body shape and structure
  • Protection – protects vital organs (e.g. skull protects the brain)
  • Movement – allows muscles to pull on bones to create movement
  • Blood cell production – produces red and white blood cells in bone marrow
  • Mineral storage – stores calcium and phosphorus

Key Bones You Must Know

  • Cranium – skull
  • Clavicle – collar bone
  • Scapula – shoulder blade
  • Humerus – upper arm
  • Radius and Ulna – forearm
  • Pelvis – hips
  • Femur – thigh
  • Tibia and Fibula – lower leg

Key Terms

Bone marrow: soft tissue inside bones where blood cells are produced

Minerals: substances such as calcium that strengthen bones

Exam Tip

When asked about the skeleton in sport, always link your answer to movement or performance, not just definitions.

Practice Question
